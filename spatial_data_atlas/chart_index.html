Global Development Dynamics (6 charts)

#01
GDP vs Life Expectancy
Animated bubble chart showing the Preston Curve across 199 countries (1990-2023). Bubble size = population, color = continent.
Takeaway: Richer countries live longer, but the relationship is logarithmic: the first $5,000 matters enormously; the next $50,000 barely moves the needle.
#02
Fertility vs Life Expectancy
Animated bubble chart tracing the demographic transition: as life expectancy rises, fertility falls. 1990-2023.
Takeaway: The world has converged dramatically: in 1990, fertility ranged from 1.2 to 7.5; by 2023, most countries cluster between 1.5 and 4.0.
#03
Child Mortality vs GDP
Animated scatter showing the inverse relationship between wealth and under-5 mortality. Log-scale GDP axis.
Takeaway: Child mortality has halved globally since 1990, but the poorest countries still lose 10x more children than the richest.
#04
Country Development Trajectories
Spaghetti plot tracing individual country paths through GDP-health space over three decades.
Takeaway: Most trajectories move right and up (richer and healthier), but conflict zones show dramatic reversals.
#05
Continental Development Trends
Area/line chart comparing continental averages across key development indicators over time.
Takeaway: Asia's rise is the dominant story: its average GDP per capita tripled in 30 years, driven by China and India.
#06
Income Distribution Shift
Animated histogram showing how the global income distribution has changed shape from 1990 to 2023.
Takeaway: The bimodal 'twin peaks' distribution of 1990 (rich vs poor) has collapsed into a single peak centered at middle income.

Animated Temporal (6 charts)

GDP Bar Chart Race
Animated bar race showing the top 15 countries by GDP per capita (PPP) racing from 1992 to 2022.
Takeaway: Ireland's meteoric rise is partly a statistical artifact of corporate tax haven effects; Qatar's lead reflects hydrocarbon wealth divided by a tiny citizen population.
#50
India Electoral Swing Animation
Animated map showing constituency-level electoral swings across Indian states.
Takeaway: Electoral swings are spatially clustered: neighboring constituencies tend to swing in the same direction, following regional political waves.
#51
Fertility Rate Collapse
Animated chart showing the dramatic decline of fertility rates worldwide.
Takeaway: Bangladesh's fertility dropped from 6.2 to 2.0 in one generation without reaching high income, disproving the 'wealth causes low fertility' hypothesis.
#52
Child Mortality Plunge
Animated visualization of the global decline in under-5 mortality rates from 1990 onward.
Takeaway: Sub-Saharan Africa saw the steepest absolute declines after 2005, driven by anti-malaria bed nets and oral rehydration therapy.
#53
Economic Convergence Race
Animated comparison showing whether poor countries are catching up to rich ones in GDP per capita.
Takeaway: Conditional convergence is real (poor countries grow faster IF they have good institutions), but unconditional convergence remains elusive.
#54
